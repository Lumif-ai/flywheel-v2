# Question Bank — Lumif.ai Discovery Interviews

Structured question templates by phase and product. Claude selects and tailors based on person, company, and opportunity type.

## Phase 1: Warm-Up Templates

Alumni: "I saw we both went through [program]. How did you end up in [industry]?"
Cold LinkedIn: "I came across your [post/article] about [topic] — it resonated with what we're seeing."
Warm intro: "[Name] mentioned you're the person to talk to about [topic]."
Conference: "I caught your session at [event] on [topic]. The point about [insight] stuck with me."

## Phase 2: Context and Role

Executives: "What does your team look like? How many focused on [function]?" / "Top 2-3 priorities for the next 12 months?" / "How do you measure success?"
Practitioners: "Walk me through a typical week." / "What tools do you rely on most?" / "If you could fix one thing about your workflow?"
Brokers: "How many clients are you managing roughly?" / "What does your client base look like?" / "How do you win new clients?"

## Phase 3: Problem Discovery by Product

### P1 (Workers Comp Premium Audit)

Carriers: "Walk me through your premium audit process start to finish." / "How many audits per year? Avg time per audit?" / "Where are the bottlenecks?" / "How do you handle classification disputes?"
Audit firms: "How do you receive audit assignments from carriers?" / "Biggest challenge in scaling volume?" / "Field vs desk audit split?" / "What tech for documentation?"
Employers: "How does the premium audit process go for you?" / "Ever had a class code dispute?" / "What records do you pull together for auditors?"

### P2 (GC Risk Compliance)

GCs: "How many active subs right now?" / "Walk me through onboarding a new sub — what does compliance look like?" / "How do you track COI expirations?" / "What happens when a sub's insurance lapses and you miss it?" / "Have you had a claim where a sub wasn't covered?" / "How many people spend time on compliance tracking?" / "Software or spreadsheets?"
Project owners: "How do you verify your GC's subs are properly insured?" / "What's your liability exposure with uninsured subs?"
Risk managers: "Most time-consuming part of sub compliance?" / "How do you enforce with non-compliant subs?" / "Have you evaluated compliance platforms?"

### P3 (Broker Risk Compliance)

Brokers: "How many construction clients do you serve?" / "Do GC clients ask for help tracking sub compliance?" / "What services do you provide beyond placing policies?" / "How do you differentiate?" / "Would offering a compliance tool help you win business?"
Agency principals: "Fastest growing line?" / "Demand for value-added services from construction clients?" / "How do you think about tech investments?"

## Phase 4: Pain Quantification

Time: "Hours per week on [process]?" / "If you got that time back, where would it go?"
Money: "Total cost per year — people, tools, opportunity cost?" / "Cost when something goes wrong?"
Risk: "Worst case if [process] fails?" / "Has it happened? What was the impact?" / "E&O or liability exposure?"
Frequency: "How often does [problem] come up?" / "Getting better or worse over time?"

Vitamin vs Painkiller classifier:
- Annoying but manageable = Vitamin
- Urgent, costly, keeps them up at night = Painkiller
- Already tried to solve or actively shopping = Strong Painkiller
- Shrug it off = Not a real problem for them

## Phase 5: Solution Landscape

"What tools do you use for [process] today?" / "How did you end up with that approach?" / "What works? What frustrates?" / "Have you evaluated alternatives?" / "What would the ideal solution look like?"

RULE: Never proactively mention competitor names. Let them surface organically.

## Phase 6: Value and Decision

WTP: "If a tool could [solve problem], what would that be worth?" / "How would you think about ROI?"
Process: "What would the evaluation process look like?" / "Who else involved in the decision?" / "Budget cycle?"
Timeline: "Looking to address soon or longer-term?" / "What needs to be true to move this quarter?"

## Phase 7: Close and Expand

Referrals: "Who else in your network deals with similar challenges?" / "Others at [company] with a different angle?"
Product interest (only if pain was clear): "We're building something that might help. Open to seeing a demo?" / "Can I keep you in the loop?"
Close: "This has been incredibly helpful. I'll send a follow-up with [promise]. Is email best?"

## Selection Rules

1. Pick 3-5 questions per active phase
2. Mark top 2 per phase as must-ask with a star
3. Tailor every question with actual names, companies, tools
4. Sequence: broad before specific, easy before hard
5. Add follow-up prompts for key questions
6. Read the room — go deeper where enthusiasm is high
