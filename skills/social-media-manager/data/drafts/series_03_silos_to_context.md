## LINKEDIN DRAFT (Hook A)

Our meeting prep automation didn't know we'd already spoken to three people at the same company. Our outreach automation didn't know we'd just learned their biggest pain point on a call last week.

Both worked great individually. That was the problem.

We'd built 8 automations in Claude Code. Meeting prep, outreach research, call intelligence, investor updates. Each one saved real time. Each one lived in its own world.

Great call with an industry expert. Learned something that changed how we should pitch to a whole category of companies. That insight sat in a transcript. Our outreach kept sending the old angle.

Prepped for a meeting. Spent 10 minutes researching a company we'd already met with twice. The prep tool didn't know because that context was in a different workflow.

8 tools. 8 islands. We were still the bridge.

The fix took days, not weeks. We built a shared context store that every automation reads from and writes back to. Nothing fancy for the user. It just works in the background. But now when we process a meeting, the next prep for that company already knows what happened. When outreach runs, it pulls from everything we've learned, not just what was in the outreach tool.

Everything interconnected. Meeting 5 with a company runs on top of 4 previous meetings, 3 outreach touchpoints, and an industry signal from two weeks ago.

The gap between isolated automations and connected ones was the biggest unlock we've had. Not even close.

Are your automations talking to each other?
