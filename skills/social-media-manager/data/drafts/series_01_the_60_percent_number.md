## LINKEDIN DRAFT (Hook A)

Building a company means doing everything. The part nobody talks about is how much of "everything" is the same process you did last week for a different person.

Different prospect, same 30-40 minutes pulling up their LinkedIn, going through their website, figuring out the pitch, getting context on where things stand. On the other hand, when signing contracts with vendors, customers, advisors, etc. contract, same clause-by-clause review.

You as a founder are compounding. You're sharper, you read situations faster, you know the market better than you did six months ago. But the process around you is flat. Same manual research. Same context-building from scratch because last month's prep lives in a doc you'll never reopen.

Sure, you get faster. Meeting 50 is quicker than meeting 1. You know where to look, you skip the dead ends. But the improvement plateaus. And the number of meetings doesn't. That gap is the Admin Tax.

We hit this wall hard. Three co-founders prepping 8-10 meetings a week, each one starting from zero. As part of improving this, we started exploring Claude Code to reduce the manual work and make the process compound instead of repeat. Got back 2-3 hours a day.

More on how in the next post.
