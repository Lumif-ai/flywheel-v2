That's not someone you pitch product features to. That changes the entire conversation. And we didn't research any of it manually.

Last post I wrote about the Admin Tax. Meeting prep was our worst example. We built a meeting prep skill in Claude Code that changed how our founding team walks into calls.

Not going to describe what it does in the abstract. Here's what it actually did last month.

A customer we'd spoken to twice before. The briefing pulled together both previous conversations and noticed something: each call had revealed a deeper layer of pain. What started as a simple request had expanded into problems across five different parts of their operations. It mapped the expansion and suggested we reposition the entire engagement before the call started.

After 15+ conversations across different prospects, patterns started emerging that no individual prep would have caught. Four separate contacts asked about the same integration. Three mentioned the same workflow pain. We didn't plan a product pivot around that. The data just surfaced it.

Before: Anoothi, Nandita and I would sit together for 30-40 minutes before each call. Research the person, go through what happened, figure out the angle.

Now: everyone gets a briefing PDF, reads it in 10 minutes, wraps their own POV around it. Three different perspectives on the same foundation. We walk in sharper with a third of the prep time.
