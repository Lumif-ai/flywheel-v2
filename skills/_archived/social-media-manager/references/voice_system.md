# Voice System

## Role Lenses

Each role changes what you notice, what you share, and your authority signal.

### Lens Definitions

| Role | Primary Lens | What You Talk About | Authority Signal |
|------|-------------|---------------------|------------------|
| CEO/Founder | Vision + decisions | Why we exist, market bets, pivots, fundraising learnings, hiring philosophy | "I bet the company on X because..." |
| CTO | Architecture + tradeoffs | Tech stack decisions, build vs. buy, scaling, engineering culture | "We chose X over Y because at our scale..." |
| CPO | Customer + product | User insights, feature decisions, what we killed, product-market fit | "Customers kept asking for X but actually needed Y..." |
| COO | Systems + execution | Process design, hiring, team scaling, metrics frameworks | "We went from 2 to 20 and here's what broke..." |
| VP Eng | Craft + team | Engineering practices, tech debt, dev productivity, mentorship | "Here's how we reduced deploy time by 80%..." |

### What Each Role Does NOT Talk About

| Role | Avoid |
|------|-------|
| CEO | Implementation details, technical benchmarks |
| CTO | Revenue numbers, sales strategy |
| CPO | Infrastructure, code quality |
| COO | Deep tech, product vision specifics |
| VP Eng | Business strategy, customer conversations |

### Combined Lenses (Intersection Posts)

The best founder posts live at the intersection of roles:

- **CEO + CTO:** "I'm the founder writing the code. Here's why I stayed technical instead of hiring a CTO."
- **CEO + CPO:** "I killed our most-requested feature because the unit economics didn't work."
- **CTO + CPO:** "Our users wanted a dashboard. Our architecture couldn't support real-time. Here's the product compromise nobody noticed."
- **CEO + CTO + CPO (triple):** "I talked to the customer, designed the solution, and wrote the code. Here's what I shipped this week."

### Multi-Hat Reality (Early-Stage Founders)

- Support primary role + secondary roles: "I'm CEO but I also do all the product work"
- Tag each post with which hat you're wearing
- Balance tracking: "You've posted 4 CEO-vision posts in a row. Consider a CPO-lens post."
- Credibility guardrails: if posting as CTO about distributed systems at scale but you're a solo founder, frame as learning/exploration, not authority

### Cross-Lens Opportunities

When mining content, tag raw ideas with ALL applicable lenses. During drafting, ask: "This touches both product and tech. Post as CEO combining both, or split into two posts?"

## Voice Registers

Posts should sound like they were typed on a phone between meetings. Not sloppy — raw.

### The Four Registers

| Register | Sounds Like | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Workshop Dispatch | Texting a cofounder | "just spent 3 hours debugging a PDF parser. turns out scanned docs from 2008 have metadata that lies about their encoding. nobody warns you about this." |
| Bar Conversation | Telling a story to a friend | "A VC asked me what our moat is. I said 'we actually talk to subcontractors.' He didn't get it. That's the moat." |
| Honest Retrospective | Journaling out loud | "6 months in. Revenue is growing but I'm not sure we're building the right thing. Here's what's bothering me." |
| Excited Builder | Showing someone what you made | "Ok this is cool — we got the compliance check from 45 minutes to 90 seconds. Let me show you how." |

### Style Rules

**Do:**
- Short sentences. Fragments even.
- Line breaks after every thought (LinkedIn algorithm + readability)
- Lowercase where natural: "we shipped it. broke in production. fixed it by 2am."
- Real numbers over rounded: "37 customers" not "nearly 40 customers"
- Start mid-story: "So this customer calls me at 9pm..."
- End abruptly or with a genuine question, never a motivational bow

**Don't (AI Tells — banned patterns):**
- Em dashes everywhere
- "Here's the thing:" or "Here's what I learned:"
- Perfect parallel structure in lists
- "And that's why I believe..."
- Three-part frameworks with alliteration
- Semicolons (nobody texts with semicolons)
- "Excited to announce" / "Thrilled to share"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Let me break it down..."
- "The truth is..."
- Wrapping up with lessons learned in a neat bow

### The Litmus Test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a conference talk, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say over coffee to another founder, ship it.

## Voice Profile Structure

Stored in memory, updated over time:

```
role:
  primary: CEO/Founder
  secondary: [CPO, CTO]
  stage: early-stage (pre-Series A)

polish_level: 2  (1=typed on phone, 5=ghostwritten thought piece)

tone_dials:  (per role, scale 1-5)
  CEO:  technical_depth=2, vulnerability=4, contrarian=4, data=2
  CTO:  technical_depth=5, vulnerability=2, contrarian=3, data=4
  CPO:  technical_depth=3, vulnerability=3, contrarian=3, data=3

preferred_registers: [workshop_dispatch, bar_conversation]

ai_tells_banned: [em dashes, "here's the thing", parallel lists, motivational closers, semicolons, "excited to announce"]

hook_preference: question  (question | statement | mid-story | statistic)
```

## Brand Voice Strategy for Technical Founders

### Recommended Primary Tone: "Builder-in-Chief"
You're in the trenches, sharing real decisions with real numbers. Not thought leadership from the sidelines — dispatches from the workshop.

### Recommended Secondary Tone: "Industry Insider"
You see what others miss because you're talking to customers daily.

### Avoid: "Guru/Advisor"
You're not teaching from a podium. You're sharing from the workshop.

### Brand Signature Discovery
Over time, the skill identifies your highest-performing intersection and suggests your brand lane. Example: "Your sweet spot is technical-founder-meets-customer-insight. That's your brand lane."
