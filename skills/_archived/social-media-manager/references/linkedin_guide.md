# LinkedIn Platform Guide

## Algorithm & Formatting

### What Gets Reach
- Line breaks after every sentence (forces "see more" click, boosts engagement signal)
- First line is 80% of the post — hook must stop the scroll
- Posts with 1-2 hashtags outperform 5+ hashtags
- Native content > links (LinkedIn suppresses external URLs)
- Comments in first hour matter most for distribution
- Carousel/document posts get 2-3x reach vs. text-only
- Polls get massive algorithmic boost (easy engagement signal)

### Formatting Rules
- Max ~3000 characters (aim for 1200-1800 for best engagement)
- One thought per line, blank line between thoughts
- No bold/italic in feed (only in articles) — use CAPS sparingly for emphasis
- Emojis as bullet points are acceptable but don't overdo
- If including a link, put it in the FIRST COMMENT, not the post body

### Post Structure (LinkedIn-Native)

```
[HOOK — 1-2 lines that stop the scroll]

[TENSION — the problem, the surprise, the conflict]

[BODY — 3-5 short paragraphs with the story/insight]

[PAYOFF — what you learned, what changed]

[CTA — question to audience OR "follow for more" equivalent]
```

### Hook Patterns That Work (Non-Generic)
- Mid-story start: "So this customer calls me at 9pm on a Friday."
- Contrarian statement: "We turned down our biggest prospect last week."
- Specific number: "37 subcontractors. 4 hours. 0 compliance violations."
- Question: "What's the most expensive bug you've ever shipped?"
- Confession: "I've been lying to myself about product-market fit."

### What to Avoid on LinkedIn
- External links in post body (kills reach)
- Tagging people who won't engage (looks desperate)
- "I'm humbled to announce..." (performative)
- Engagement bait: "Like if you agree, comment if you disagree"
- Posting more than once per day (cannibalizes your own reach)

## Content Types by Performance

| Type | Reach | Engagement | Best For |
|------|-------|------------|----------|
| Personal story + lesson | High | High | Brand building |
| Contrarian take | Very High | Very High | Thought leadership (risky) |
| Behind-the-scenes build | Medium-High | High | Developer/founder audience |
| Data/insight with chart | Medium | Medium | Credibility |
| Carousel (PDF) | Very High | Medium | Frameworks, tutorials |
| Poll | Very High | Very High | Engagement, market research |
| Job posting narrative | Medium | Low | Hiring |
| Company announcement | Low | Low | Required but don't over-index |

## Carousel Posts (PDF Format)

LinkedIn carousels are PDF uploads displayed as swipeable slides:
- 1st slide: bold headline + visual hook
- 2-8 slides: one insight per slide, large text
- Last slide: CTA + follow prompt
- Optimal: 6-10 slides
- File format: PDF (not images)
- Design: clean, high contrast, brand colors, minimal text per slide

## Posting Schedule

Best times (general — calibrate with user's own data):
- Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am local time
- Avoid: Friday afternoon, weekends, Monday morning
- Consistency > timing — same 2-3 days per week is better than chasing optimal hours
