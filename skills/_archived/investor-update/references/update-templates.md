# Investor Update Templates & Style Guide

Derived from 5 months of Lumif.ai investor updates (Aug 2025 - Jan 2026).

## Tone & Voice

- **Direct and confident** — no hedging, no filler. Lead with impact.
- **Founder-to-investor conversational** — casual greeting ("Hey everyone"), warm but professional
- **Strategic framing** — every section connects to the bigger picture
- **Signal > noise** — specific proof points, not vague progress claims
- **Forward-looking** — every section ends with what's next or why it matters

## Opening

Always start with a warm, brief greeting:
```
Hey everyone,
Here's our [Month] update covering [key themes].
```

## Standard Sections (in order)

### 1. What Does Lumif.ai Do? (Company Description)
- 2-3 paragraph elevator pitch
- Updated as positioning evolves
- Include market size signal ("$200B+ industry")
- End with current focus and broader vision
- This section evolves as the company pivots — always reflect current positioning

### 2. Key Wins & Progress
- **Lead with the biggest win** — pilot live, revenue, partnership, conference signal
- Bullet or numbered format for clarity
- Include specific details: company size, deal value, geography
- Each win should have a "why this matters" signal
- Sub-sections by theme (Pilots, Partnerships, Customer Conversations, On-the-Ground)

### 3. Product Milestones
- **Table format** with columns: Component | Status | Description
- Status values: Completed, In Progress, Live, Expanding, Deprioritized
- 4-6 rows typical
- Brief but specific descriptions
- Link to demos/videos when available

### 4. Strategic Shifts & Learnings
- What changed in thinking and why
- Back up with data (X interviews, Y conversations)
- Frame as sharpening, not pivoting (unless it truly is a pivot)
- Include "what we're NOT doing" — shows strategic discipline

### 5. Team & Advisors (when applicable)
- New hires with bar-setting context
- Advisor additions with credentials and value they bring
- Team offsite outcomes
- Keep brief — 2-3 sentences per update

### 6. Financial Snapshot
- Always link to Papermark (don't paste raw numbers in update)
- Standard bullet list:
  - Monthly burn rate
  - Runway
  - Estimated Contract Value from pilots
  - Cash on hand and reserves

### 7. Next Steps / Next 60 Days
- 3-5 concrete, time-bound objectives
- Action-oriented language ("Secure", "Launch", "Define", "Finalize")
- Tied to current strategy, not wish-list items

### 8. Asks
- **Always include asks** — this is the whole point of investor updates
- Be specific about who you want intros to (title, industry, company type)
- If traveling, include dates and locations
- Frame as "Introductions to..." not generic "help us"

### 9. Closing Note (optional, used in some months)
- 2-3 sentences max
- Summarize the month's theme
- Preview next month's focus
- End with gratitude

## Formatting Rules

- Use **H2 (##)** for main sections, **H3 (###)** for sub-sections
- Bold key phrases within paragraphs for scannability
- Tables for product milestones and structured comparisons
- Bullet lists for wins, asks, and next steps
- No emojis in the body (occasional use in conference/event callouts is OK)
- Keep total length to 1-2 pages (500-800 words excluding company description)

| Section | Frequency | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Company Description | Every month | Updated as positioning evolves |
| Key Wins | Every month | Always lead with strongest signal |

| Team/Advisors | As needed | When new hires or advisors onboard |
| Financial Snapshot | Every month | Always link to Papermark |
| Next Steps | Every month | Time-bound, 60-day horizon |
| Asks | Every month | Specific and actionable |

| Closing Note | Optional | Thematic summary |
