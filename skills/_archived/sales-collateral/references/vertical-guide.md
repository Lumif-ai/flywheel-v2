## Existing Vertical Knowledge

### Construction (UK)

**Insurance types**: Employers' Liability (statutory), Public Liability, Professional Indemnity, Contractors' All Risks, indemnity clauses, non-vitiation wording, additional insured endorsements

**Regulatory**: HSE for health and safety. No direct insurance regulator but contractual obligations enforced through litigation. Due diligence defence matters when claims arise.

**Compliance ecosystem**: Constructionline, CHAS, SafeContractor for prequalification. Procore, Aconex for project management. Broker-mediated document collection common.

**Terminology**: Main contractor (buyer), trade contractors/subcontractors (supply chain), subcontract (contract document), compliance manager or risk manager (role), Finance Director (budget holder)

**Nightmare scenario**: Lapsed EL on a live site, wrong additional insured endorsement, missing non-vitiation clause. A claim arises and the main contractor can't demonstrate they verified the sub's coverage against the contract.

**Headline scenarios (proven)**: "Lapsed Employers' Liability on a live site. Wrong additional insured endorsement. Missing non-vitiation clause."

**Named tools to reference**: "Constructionline and CHAS prequalify suppliers."

**Due diligence framing**: "When a claim arises, you can show exactly what was checked, when, and against which clause."

**Promise box stakeholders**: "One dashboard for your compliance manager, your broker, and your client."

**CTA**: "...for your projects."

### Water Utilities (UK)

**Insurance types**: Employers' Liability (statutory), Public Liability, Professional Indemnity, Contractors' All Risks, Environmental Liability, pollution cover, contamination indemnity

**Regulatory**: Ofwat (economic regulation), Environment Agency (environmental enforcement), Drinking Water Inspectorate (water quality), HSE (health and safety). EA enforcement is the primary fear for insurance gaps.

**Compliance ecosystem**: Achilles UVDB for prequalification (with UVDB Verify for SHEQ audits against ISO 45001, 14001, 9001). SafeContractor. SHEQ stack covers accreditations and permits. UVDB confirms insurance exists at registration but doesn't do ongoing contract-to-coverage reconciliation.

**Terminology**: Water company or asset owner (buyer), contractors or supply chain partners (supply chain), framework agreement or contract (contract document), compliance team or procurement (role), Finance Director (budget holder). "AMP8" = 2025-2030 investment cycle.

**Nightmare scenario**: Contractor working near a watercourse without adequate pollution cover. EA investigates, finds the water company had no systematic process for verifying contractor insurance against contract requirements.

**Headline scenarios (proven)**: "Lapsed Employers' Liability. Missing pollution cover near a watercourse. Inadequate PI on a design-build contract."

**Named tools to reference**: "Achilles UVDB is where your team records that a supplier's insurance was checked."

**Due diligence framing**: "Full audit trail demonstrating systematic contract-to-coverage verification, ready for Environment Agency or Ofwat review."

**Tier cascade**: Tier 1 alliance partners and Tier 2 subcontractors. Water company exposed to Tier 2 gaps they often can't see.

**Promise box stakeholders**: "One dashboard for Ofwat, Environment Agency, and internal governance."

**CTA**: "...for your supply chain."

### Construction (US)

**Insurance types**: Workers' Compensation, Commercial General Liability (CGL), Professional Liability, Umbrella/Excess, Contractors' All Risks. Key endorsements: Additional Insured (CG 20 10 vs CG 20 33/37), Waiver of Subrogation, Primary & Non-Contributory.

**Regulatory**: OSHA for safety. No direct insurance regulator but contractual enforcement through litigation. CCIP/OCIP wrap programmes are common (owner-led).

**Compliance ecosystem**: COI tracking (myCOI/illumend, Jones, TrustLayer, CertFocus). NCCI for experience modifiers (EMod). OSHA safety ratings. No UK-style UVDB equivalent.

**Terminology**: General contractor (buyer), subcontractors (supply chain), MSA or subcontract agreement (contract document), risk manager (role), CFO (budget holder). Revenue not turnover. Dollars not pounds.

**Nightmare scenario**: GL claim on a project, sub's coverage had lapsed months earlier. $250K+ in direct losses and legal fees.

**Named tools to reference**: "COI trackers check fields. CLM tools store contracts. Pre-qual platforms score vendors."
