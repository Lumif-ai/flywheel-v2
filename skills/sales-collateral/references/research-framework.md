# Vertical Research Framework

Detailed checklist for researching a new vertical before creating sales collateral.

## Pre-Writing Research Checklist

Complete ALL sections before writing any content. Each section should be populated with specific, named examples, not generic descriptions.

### 1. Industry Pain Mapping

| Question | What You Need |
|----------|---------------|
| What's the high-stakes failure scenario? | A specific, visceral scenario the buyer will immediately recognise (e.g., "a contractor on a live site with lapsed coverage") |
| What are 3 different failure types? | For the headline. Each should hit a different fear centre (financial, regulatory, reputational) |
| What's the financial exposure? | Realistic figure for a single failure. Not the worst case, but a credible mid-range incident. |
| What's the regulatory consequence? | Which regulator, what enforcement action, what penalties? |
| What's the reputational consequence? | Client loss, market reputation, insurance premium impact? |

### 2. Existing Tool Ecosystem

| Question | What You Need |
|----------|---------------|
| What prequalification tools do they use? | Named products (e.g., Achilles UVDB, Constructionline, CHAS, ISNetworld, Avetta) |
| What document management do they use? | Named products (e.g., Procore, Aconex, SharePoint, SAP) |
| What do these tools do WELL? | Be specific. You need to respect these tools in your positioning. |
| What gap do they leave? | The specific task that still requires manual human work despite having these tools |
| Is this a "system of record" gap? | Does a human still have to do the analysis and key in the result? |

### 3. Terminology Map

| Generic Term | What This Vertical Calls It |
|---|---|
| The buyer | (e.g., main contractor, asset owner, operator) |
| The suppliers | (e.g., trade contractors, supply chain partners, vendors) |
| The contract | (e.g., subcontract, framework agreement, MSA, SLA) |
| The compliance role | (e.g., risk manager, compliance officer, procurement) |
| The budget holder | (e.g., Finance Director, CFO, VP Operations) |
| The regulator | (e.g., HSE, EA, Ofwat, CQC, ORR, OSHA) |

### 4. Regulatory Landscape

| Question | What You Need |
|----------|---------------|
| Who regulates? | Named body with jurisdiction |
| What triggers enforcement? | Incidents, audits, complaints, reporting failures? |
| What does "due diligence defence" look like? | Can the buyer demonstrate reasonable steps to avoid the failure? |
| Are there investment/compliance cycles? | (e.g., AMP8 for water, CP7 for rail, annual renewal cycles) |
| What audit trail would a regulator expect? | What documentation proves systematic compliance? |

### 5. Buyer Persona

| Question | What You Need |
|----------|---------------|
| Who has the problem day-to-day? | Title and typical team size |
| Who has budget authority? | Title (this is who the doc might get forwarded to) |
| What keeps them up at night? | The specific fear, not the generic "compliance risk" |
| What would make them forward this doc internally? | The one insight or number that makes them think "I need to show this to [boss]" |
| How do they talk about this problem? | Their language, not vendor language |

## Example Research Output: UK Water Utilities

### 1. Industry Pain
- **High-stakes scenario**: Contractor working near a watercourse without adequate pollution cover
- **Three headline failures**: Lapsed Employers' Liability, missing pollution cover near a watercourse, inadequate Professional Indemnity on a design-build contract
- **Financial exposure**: GBP 200K+ for a single uninsured environmental incident
- **Regulatory consequence**: EA enforcement notice, potential prosecution, Ofwat scrutiny
- **Reputational consequence**: Media coverage of contamination event, client trust erosion

### 2. Existing Tools
- **Prequalification**: Achilles UVDB (with Verify for SHEQ audits against ISO 45001/14001/9001)
- **Safety**: SafeContractor
- **What they do well**: Confirm suppliers meet baseline standards at registration
- **The gap**: Nobody verifies that coverage still matches contract requirements month to month. UVDB is a system of record. A human still manually reviews policies and keys in results.
- **System of record gap**: Yes. The tool stores what someone concluded, but a person had to do all the analytical work.

### 3. Terminology
- Buyer = Water company / asset owner
- Suppliers = Contractors / supply chain partners
- Contract = Framework agreement / contract
- Compliance role = Compliance team / procurement
- Budget holder = Finance Director
- Regulator = Ofwat / Environment Agency

### 4. Regulatory
- Ofwat (economic), EA (environmental), DWI (water quality), HSE (safety)
- EA investigates after environmental incidents; looks for evidence of systematic compliance processes
- AMP8 (2025-2030) driving increased contractor volumes and compliance pressure
- Due diligence = demonstrable systematic verification process with audit trail

### 5. Buyer Persona
- Day-to-day: Supply chain compliance team (distributed across procurement, commercial, SHEQ)
- Budget: Finance Director or Head of Procurement
- Fear: EA investigates an incident, asks to see how contractor insurance compliance was managed, and finds emails and spreadsheets
- Forward trigger: "This is the first time I've seen someone describe exactly what we're NOT doing"
- Their language: "Supply chain assurance", "contractor management", not "insurance compliance automation"

## Research Sources

When researching a new vertical, prioritise:
1. **Regulator websites** — for enforcement data, guidance documents, compliance expectations
2. **Industry body publications** — trade associations, professional institutes
3. **Sector-specific prequalification systems** — their websites explain what they check and what they don't
4. **Job postings for compliance roles** — reveals what tasks are manual, what tools are used, what the pain points are
5. **Insurance broker industry reports** — Marsh, Aon, Zurich sector reports contain useful benchmarks

Avoid:
- Competitor marketing blogs (directional at best, biased by definition)
- Generic "compliance automation" content (too broad to be useful)
- Analyst reports behind paywalls (can't verify or cite)
